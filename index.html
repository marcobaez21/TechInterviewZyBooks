Finding the Slope and Y-Intercept of a Line Given Two Points

Algebraically

Given the points (1, 2) and (5, 10) to find the slope we must complete the following steps:
  • Plug in the points to this equation: (y2-y1)/(x2-x1)
  • Doing so gives us this (10-2)/(5-1)
  • Simplifying (10-2)/(5-1) gives us a slope of 2
Given the points (1, 2) and (5, 10) with a slope of 2 to find the Y-intercept we must complete the following steps:
  • Plug in one of the given points and the slope to the equation y=mx+b where m is the slope and b is the Y-intercept
  • Plugging in the point (1, 2) and the slope of 2 results in 2=2x1+b
  • Simplifying 2=2x1+b results in b=0 or a Y-intercept of 0

Graphically


Given the points (2, 2) and (4, 3) to find the slope graphically we must complete the following steps, as seen above:
  • Plot both points and draw a line connecting both of them
  • Count the x value's distance between the two points, in this case it is 2
  • Count the y value's distance between the two points, in this case it is 1
  • Plugging into the equation m = Δy / Δx gives a slope of 1/2
Given the points (2, 2) and (4, 3) to find the Y-Intercept graphically we must complete the following steps, as seen above:
  • Plot both points and draw a line connecting both of them
  • Continue drawing this line until the line hits the Y axis
  • The point where the line hits the Y axis is the Y-Intercept. In this case it is 1
